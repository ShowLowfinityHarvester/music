I Am Music

Playboi Carti
8/10
WSLF Radio Reviews Apr 17, 2025

Review

Playboi Carti's *I Am Music* (released March 14, 2025) is a bold, sprawling statement that both builds on and departs from the chaotic energy of his 2020 album *Whole Lotta Red*. With 30 tracks spanning 77 minutes, the album is a sonic journey through Carti's evolving sound, blending his signature rage-rap style with poppier, more cinematic elements. Tracks like "POP OUT" and "CRUSH" hit hard with blown-out synths and aggressive energy, while songs like "Rather Lie" (featuring The Weeknd) show a softer, more melodic side. The production, handled by heavyweights like F1lthy, Metro Boomin, and Ye, is consistently stellar, creating a cohesive yet varied soundscape.

The album's length is both a strength and a weakness—while it offers something for every Carti fan, it can feel bloated at times, with some tracks lacking the punch of his best work. Features from Travis Scott, Kendrick Lamar, and Young Thug add star power, but Carti's presence remains the focal point, his voice weaving through distorted beats with an almost hypnotic quality. *I Am Music* feels like Carti declaring himself a genre unto himself, a sentiment echoed in the album's title and his own statements about its importance. It's a polarizing album that rewards repeated listens, even if it doesn't always hit the mark.

Why This Review?

We approached this review with a focus on Carti's growth as an artist since *Whole Lotta Red*. Having followed his career, we wanted to highlight how *I Am Music* reflects his ambition to push boundaries while staying true to his roots in trap and rage-rap. We noted the album's cinematic quality because it stood out as a defining trait—Carti isn't just making songs; he's crafting an experience. We also considered the mixed reception, like the AI deepfake controversies around tracks like "Rather Lie," to give a balanced view of the album's impact.

Rating Context

Our review leans positive but acknowledges flaws, as we believe *I Am Music* is a significant step for Carti, even if it doesn't always land perfectly. The album's length and occasional lack of focus were points of criticism, but its highs—like the production and Carti's vocal experimentation—are undeniable. We rated it an 8/10 for its influence on modern hip-hop, where Carti continues to be a trendsetter, as seen in its #1 debut on the Billboard 200 with 298,000 units moved in its first week.
