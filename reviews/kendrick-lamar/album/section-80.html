Section.80

Kendrick Lamar
8/10
Released on Jul 2, 2011 | Reviewed on Oct 15, 2012

Strong Start for a Compton Kid

We are kickin off the music reviews series, and what better way to start than with Kendrick Lamar’s official debut album, Section.80? Spanning 16 tracks and clocking in at just under an hour, it's clear this isn't your typical rapper on the West Coast flexin and acting tough, dudes dropping real bars talkin about the struggles of growing up and stuff. The beats on this thing are straight up fire, with that futuristic 90's alternative rap vibe that takes you back in time. Tracks like A.D.H.D and Rigamortus go hard with their sick flows and dope production, and Kendrick's delivery got me hooked. It's as if I'm in LA myself experiencing everything Kendrick is saying.

Rating

Now it's time to rate this album. I am gonna introduce my own rating system, called the Doutree Richter Scale. it’s like a regular x/10 scale, but we talkin shakin floors, active clubs, and how hard the beats are hittin. The solid and impressive beats, kendrick's flow, and the fact that this is a debut album lands this album an 8 on the Doutree Richter Scale. It shakes up the floor quite a bit, what I like to see from a debut album.

Favorite Tracks: A.D.H.D, Chapter 12

Least Favorite Track: Tammy's song (Her evils)
