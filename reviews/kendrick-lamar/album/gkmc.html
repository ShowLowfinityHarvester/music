good kid, m.A.A.d city

Kendrick Lamar
9/10
Released on Oct 22, 2012 | Reviewed on Oct 29, 2012

A Compton story that hits hard

It's time to review Kendrick Lamar's second studio project, good kid, m.A.A.d city. Spanning 17 tracks counting the deluxe tracks and clocking in at just about an hour, it is clear that this is more than just an album, it's a whole entire story. This album takes you straight into Compton, painting this vivid picture of growing up with all the pressure, temptation, and drama that comes with it. The storytelling is raw, with topics about dodging gang life, dealing with peer pressure, and tryna find his way. Tracks like The Art of Peer Pressure and m.A.A.d city hit you with Kendrick's sharp ass bars that make you feel like you are riding the streets of Compton.

Rating

I'm gonna start off by saying that this project is a huge step up from the previous project [Section.80](https://www.wslfradio.org/music/reviews/kendrick-lamar/album/section-80), and possibly the best rap album of the year. Kendrick gives you this well put together story that feels like a short film. Aside from the skits that mess up the flow a little bit, this is a near perfect album that shakes up the floor a lot, which lands this album a solid 9 on the Doutree Richter Scale.

Favorite Tracks: Money Trees, m.A.A.d city, The Art of Peer Pressure

Least Favorite Track: Sherane a.k.a Master Splinter’s Daughter
